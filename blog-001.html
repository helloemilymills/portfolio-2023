How I Made a Racial Diversity Poster in Photoshop



THE BRIEF

Create an image set in the 1950s that tells a compelling story. It must include three symbols that reference the 1950s.


THE SYMBOLS

I chose Atomic Design (the starburst clock), colour TV, and the first Barbie doll (in the girl's hand).


THE STORY

I wanted to tell a story about embracing diversity, and how it is a strength rather than a point of division. I like to think of a diamond in the sunshine as it refracts the light into the full spectrum of colour. In this analogy, each one of those colours represents the individuals in the human race. (Not just in terms of skin colour, but personality, purpose, strengths & weaknessess. Everything that makes us unique. But for the purpose of this image, the message is focused on race.) We are beautiful on our own, but our beauty is even stronger when we embrace our diversity.


CONCEPT SKETCH



Through this project, I discovered that sketching is a really useful part of ideation, even if your drawing skills are basic. I was pretty set on a different layout, until pushed to create more sketches (this was number 3), which led me to a better idea than what I had originally. Always try to push yourself beyond your first idea :)

Text

Fonts used were Uberhand Pro Black for "Stronger", and Quimby Gubernatorial for "Together", both Adobe Fonts. The line embellishments & diamond illustration I created myself, and assembled in Adobe Illustrator.


Original Content

The girl is a picture I took of myself, styled to appear younger and within the 1950s theme.
